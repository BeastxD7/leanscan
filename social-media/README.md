# LeanScan — Social Media Playbook

*For your X / Twitter build-in-public arc.*

---

## The voice

You are an **indie dev building LeanScan in public.** Not a calm wellness brand. Not a corporate handle. A real person making real decisions, sharing the work.

**Tone:**
- First-person ("I shipped" not "we launched")
- Specific numbers ("Bedrock costs $0.012 per scan" not "AI is expensive")
- Honest about what's broken
- Show, don't tell — every other post should have a screenshot / video / code block
- Mix tech depth with product story
- Self-deprecating when something flops, confident when something works
- No corporate hashtag spam. One hashtag max per tweet, often zero.

**What this voice is NOT:**
- Not "growth hacker bro" (no "the ONE trick to 10x your followers")
- Not "wellness influencer" (no "your daily reminder that…")
- Not "VC thought leader" (no "as someone who's evaluated 1000+ founders…")
- Not "wholesome motivation" (no "remember to be kind to yourself")

---

## Cadence

**Sustainable target: 1 tweet/day + 1 thread per week.**

Don't try to post 5x/day for the first month. That's the most common mistake. You'll burn out, drop to zero, lose momentum, and feel worse than if you'd never started.

Better pattern:

| Day | Post type | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Progress tweet (what shipped over the weekend) + screenshot | 5 min |
| Tue | Tech / decision tweet (a design or engineering call I made and why) | 10 min |
| Wed | "Building today" — a question or a screenshot of WIP | 3 min |
| Thu | Number / milestone (signups, scans, credits issued, whatever you have) | 5 min |
| Fri | A weekly thread (5–8 tweets) summarizing the week | 30 min |
| Sat | Lighter — funny screenshot, observation, response to a quote tweet | 3 min |
| Sun | Off, or a "what's next this week" tweet | 3 min |

That's 1 hour/week. Sustainable. If a tweet pops, double down for a few days on the topic. If a tweet flops, move on — don't delete, just keep going.

## What goes viral in build-in-public

Patterns that have worked for indie devs in this space (Sahil Lavingia, Pieter Levels, Marc Lou, Damon Chen, etc.):

1. **Numbers with context.** "$3,200 MRR after 4 months. Here's what I learned." Numbers without context flop. Context without numbers flops. Both together — that's the formula.
2. **Before/after screenshots.** "Week 1" vs "Week 8" visual progress.
3. **The 30-second demo video.** No audio, no editing — just hands-and-screen showing the magic moment.
4. **The "I just shipped X" tweet with a screenshot of the deploy or the feature working.** Casual, raw.
5. **Honest failure tweets.** "Spent 6 hours debugging this. Was a typo. Send help." People love these because they're real.
6. **Counter-intuitive observations.** "Turns out users don't want a smart AI. They want a fast AI."
7. **The thread that breaks down a hard problem.** "Here's how I built protein estimation from a photo in 5 seconds, and why it almost didn't work."

Patterns that DON'T work for indie devs:
- "Hot takes" about other people's products
- "Inspiration porn" with stock photos
- Long quote tweets agreeing with someone you don't know
- Generic "follow for more" CTAs
- "Founder advice" before you have founder-level traction

## Where this fits in the bigger plan

X is your **distribution moat** while you have zero budget. Every 1000 followers is roughly equivalent to a $100 ad spend in terms of attention reach (very rough, but directionally correct).

You're not trying to build 100k followers. You're trying to build 1000 followers who actually care about LeanScan. From those 1000, ~50 will sign up when you launch. From those 50, ~10 will become daily users. That's a real beachhead.

**Build-in-public also helps when you eventually need:**
- VC intros — they all watch X
- Hiring — devs read your build journal before applying
- PR — journalists scour X for indie stories
- Partnerships — telehealth founders watch the space

**One specific tactical recommendation:** every tweet should serve at least one of these three goals: (a) drive a beta signup, (b) earn a reply from someone who could be a user, (c) build long-term recognition for the LeanScan brand. If a draft doesn't do any of those, don't post it.

## Three rules

1. **Never delete tweets that flop.** Algorithm penalizes deletes. Just keep going. The next post is what matters.
2. **Reply to every reply for the first 1000 followers.** Genuinely. People remember.
3. **Don't pitch.** Tell stories. Share screenshots. Talk shop. The pitch happens implicitly when the story is good.
