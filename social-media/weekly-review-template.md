# Weekly review — Friday thread template

Open this file every Friday. Fill in the blanks. Post the thread by 5pm local time. Takes 20 minutes max.

---

## Pre-flight (5 minutes)

Before writing, gather:

- [ ] What 2-3 features did I ship this week? (list, no fluff)
- [ ] What broke or what struggle defined the week?
- [ ] What numbers do I have? (signups, scans, X followers, costs, anything)
- [ ] One thing I learned this week
- [ ] One specific thing I'm doing next week

If you can't answer most of these, you didn't ship much this week. That's fine — write a "slow week, here's what I'm working on" thread instead.

## Variations

### "Slow week" version

If the week was thin, don't fake it. Post this instead:

> **1/3**
> Week [N] of LeanScan. Slower week — wrapped up [the smaller thing I shipped] and spent the rest of it thinking through [the harder problem still ahead]. Quick update — 🧵
>
> **2/3**
> What I'm wrestling with: [the actual problem]. The two paths I see are:
> - Option A: [tradeoff]
> - Option B: [tradeoff]
>
> Leaning [your direction] because [reason]. Anyone else solved this before?
>
> **3/3**
> Next week: deciding which path and shipping the result. Will report back. Replies welcome — I read every one.

Honest beats inflated. People share honest threads more than fake-momentum threads.

---

### "Just launched something big" version

If you shipped a major thing (TestFlight live, first paying user, etc.):

> **1/5**
> [The big thing] just happened with LeanScan. Sharing the moment + the back story — 🧵
>
> **2/5**
> [Specific version of the milestone — "Just sent the first TestFlight invite to a real user" / "First $5 came through tonight" / "Crossed 100 signups"]
>
> [Why this milestone mattered to you specifically]
>
> **3/5**
> The story: [3-4 sentences on the path to this milestone — what almost killed it, what worked, what surprised you]
>
> **4/5**
> What I'd tell myself 4 weeks ago: [one specific lesson — be useful, not abstract]
>
> **5/5**
> Next milestone: [the immediate next thing]
>
> Thanks to everyone who's been replying and following along — it really does compound when you're solo.
>
> [If applicable: TestFlight / signup link]

Milestone threads are the highest-engagement format. Save them for actual milestones. Inflating "I made a button" into a milestone thread will burn audience trust.

---

## Posting hygiene

- **Time of post:** Friday 4-6pm local time tends to do best for indie dev audience (after lunch break, before weekend).
- **Don't edit after posting** unless it's a typo. Edits don't propagate well to replies.
- **Pin the best one of the month** to your profile so new visitors see the strongest summary.
- **Bookmark a thread** for yourself so you can find it in 6 months and remember how week N actually went. The retrospective value is real.
