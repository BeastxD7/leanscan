# Viral hook patterns

The first line of a tweet does 90% of the work. If the opening doesn't grab someone in the first 0.5 seconds of scrolling, the rest of the tweet doesn't exist.

These patterns are stolen from posts that worked in the indie / build-in-public / dev-twitter spaces. Adapt them to LeanScan specifically — don't post the templates verbatim.

---

## Pattern 1: The specific number opener

> "$3,200 MRR after 4 months. Here's what I learned."
> "23,000 lines of code. 12 features shipped. 2 features killed."
> "Spent 4 hours debugging a regex. It was missing a backslash."
> "5,000 AI scans logged this week. Cost: $60. Insight: …"

**Why it works:** Numbers are concrete. They cut through noise. They imply you have something specific to say.

**LeanScan adaptations:**
- "$108/month is what it costs to run LeanScan's AI for 1000 users…"
- "85% of the meal photos LeanScan sees are identified correctly. The other 15% taught me…"
- "30 seconds is the median time to log a meal in MyFitnessPal. LeanScan does it in 5."

---

## Pattern 2: The "I did the thing you keep talking about" opener

> "I quit my job."
> "I deleted 4,000 lines of code today."
> "I shipped a feature, then deleted it 6 hours later."
> "I stopped tracking metrics."

**Why it works:** Implicit story tension. The reader wants to know why.

**LeanScan adaptations:**
- "I cut the barcode scanner from LeanScan's v1."
- "I deleted the streak counter before it shipped."
- "I switched LeanScan's AI provider in production today."

---

## Pattern 3: The contrarian observation

> "Streaks are a anti-pattern."
> "Most analytics dashboards are decoration."
> "The 'AI replaces engineers' take has it backwards."

**Why it works:** Punchy. Disagreement-bait. People reply to argue, replies = engagement.

**LeanScan adaptations:**
- "Calorie trackers are designed for an audience that doesn't exist."
- "Most 'AI-first' apps would be better as 'AI-second' apps."
- "Streak counters are emotional debt collection."

*Warning:* contrarian only works if you ACTUALLY believe it and can defend the take in replies. Don't post a hot take you can't back up.

---

## Pattern 4: The unfinished list

> "Tools I'm building with right now:"
> "Things I'm NOT building for v1:"
> "Mistakes from the last 4 weeks:"

**Why it works:** Lists are skimmable. The colon promises specifics.

**LeanScan adaptations:**
- "Things I cut from LeanScan v1:"
- "Decisions I'd reverse if I started over:"
- "What I'd tell myself 4 weeks ago about building this:"

---

## Pattern 5: The honest struggle

> "I'm stuck on this and don't know why."
> "Today was a bad day."
> "Spent 6 hours on a feature, deleted it at 11pm."

**Why it works:** Vulnerability earns trust. The internet rewards founders who are honest about how hard it is.

**LeanScan adaptations:**
- "Lost an entire afternoon to an Expo SDK 55 multipart upload bug."
- "Today I shipped 4 features and broke 2 of them by 4pm."
- "I keep second-guessing the protein-first hierarchy. Everyone else does calories-first."

---

## Pattern 6: The visual lead

> "Look at this:"
> "[Just the screenshot, no text]"
> "Before / after:"

**Why it works:** Twitter privileges images. A screenshot in the timeline catches the eye when a wall of text doesn't.

**LeanScan adaptations:**
- "Before / after — LeanScan home screen 4 weeks ago vs today" [side-by-side]
- "This is what the snap → save flow looks like" [video]
- "The protein ring on launch day vs after 3 redesigns" [evolution]

---

## Pattern 7: The "you've been doing X wrong"

> "Stop using `npm install --save`. It's the default. Just `npm install`."
> "If your README starts with 'TODO', you've already lost the README war."

**Why it works:** Triggers the response "wait, am I doing this wrong?" People click and read defensively.

**LeanScan adaptations:**
- "If your AI calorie tracker still makes you type the food name first, you're building it backwards."
- "Most macro trackers default to calories first. That hierarchy is wrong for anyone trying to preserve muscle."

*Warning:* don't be condescending. The line between "useful correction" and "smug founder" is thin.

---

## Pattern 8: The question that invites a reply

> "Why did you stop tracking food?"
> "What's the longest you've spent logging a single meal?"
> "What's a feature you would pay extra to NOT have in your tracker?"

**Why it works:** Replies inflate engagement way more than likes. A tweet with 50 replies and 10 likes beats one with 500 likes and 0 replies in distribution.

**LeanScan adaptations:**
- Already covered in the launch-week sequence (Day 9).
- Run one of these every ~2 weeks.

---

## Pattern 9: The thread teaser

> "I built X in N weeks. Here's everything — 🧵"
> "Lessons from $0 → $50k MRR — 🧵"
> "A complete breakdown of how Y works — 🧵"

**Why it works:** The 🧵 signal trains audiences. Threads get longer dwell time, which the algorithm rewards.

**LeanScan adaptations:**
- "I built an AI calorie tracker in 4 weeks. Here's every decision — 🧵"
- "How LeanScan estimates protein from a photo, in 7 tweets — 🧵"
- "Everything I cut from v1 of LeanScan and why — 🧵"

---

## Pattern 10: The "small thing that mattered"

> "Smallest change I made this month: …. Biggest impact."
> "Removed one word from my homepage. Conversions went up 3%."
> "Changed the default sort order. Support tickets dropped 20%."

**Why it works:** Reveals craft. People who care about quality re-share these.

**LeanScan adaptations:**
- "Changed one chip on the home screen from 'Add manually' to '+ Manual'. New-user activation went up [N]%."
- "Replaced the native iOS alert with a custom bottom sheet. Users started reading the actual confirmation text."

---

## Three rules for hooks

1. **First 7 words decide whether anyone reads tweet 2.** Spend extra time on the opening line.
2. **Don't bait without paying off.** If the hook promises a story, the tweet must deliver one. "How I built X — 🧵" with a 1-tweet thread is a betrayal.
3. **Cut clever for specific.** "Builders gonna build" is clever and forgettable. "I deleted 3 features today" is specific and memorable. Specific wins.
